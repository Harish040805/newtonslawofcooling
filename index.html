About

Newton's Cooling Law (or) Newton's Law of Cooling is a relation between Time and Temperature

It is used to determine the following things:

i) When does a body started to cool or heat
ii) When will the body cool or heat to a particular temperature
iii) In a particular scenario, whether the body will heat or cool
iv) What is the time at a particular temperature
v) Other relevant questions related to temperature and time

Important Instructions for Newton's Cooling Law Calculator

First, convert the given word problem in Newton's Law of Cooling into a suitable data format and then provide the input for the web app to get accurate results.

To ensure accurate results, please follow these instructions:

  1. Input Values: You can input any combination of values, and the calculator will solve for the missing values.
  2. Temperature Relationship: If Temperature 1 and Temperature 2 are both provided, they should both be either above or below the surrounding temperature.
  3. Cooling or Heating:
    • If Temperature 1 is greater than Temperature 2, then it means the body is cooling.
    • If Temperature 1 is less than Temperature 2, then it means the body is heating up.
  4. Time:
    • Time 1 is the initial time, which is always 0 minutes.
    • Time 2 is the time at which Temperature 2 is measured.
  5. Surrounding Temperature: The surrounding temperature is constant.
  6. Original Body Temperature: The original body temperature should be on the same side of the surrounding temperature as Temperature 1 and Temperature 2.

    7. Example: If Surrounding Temperature is 25°C, and Temperature 1 and Temperature 2 are both above 25°C, the Original Body Temperature should also be above 25°C.

  7. Cooling Rate (k): If you don't know the cooling rate, leave it blank and the calculator will calculate it for you.
  8. Calculate Button: Click the "Calculate" button to solve for the missing values.
  9. Result: The calculator will display the calculated values, including the cooling rate, time, or temperature, depending on the input values.
